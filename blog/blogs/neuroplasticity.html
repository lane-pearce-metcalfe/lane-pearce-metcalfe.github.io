Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people:

First let me lay out that a fixed mindset not only leads to stagnant but a negative learning process as the brain needs its "exercise" to keep up its neural pathways and neglecting those pathways leads to deterioration.
As well as the fact that a growth mindset leads to the brain constantly pushing itself and exercising those pathways leading to a positive learning process.

This is the idea of neuroplasticity, that your brain is able to mold and you're able to grow within whatever topic you desire just by using these simple ideas to your advantage.
Just from the simple concept of a fixed mindset leads to those pathways deteriorating and a growth mindset on the other side literally growing those pathways, it's very clear and obvious how using neuroplasticity to your advantage can benefit you!

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

As I said before, your brain needs to constantly have its "exercise" to keep those neural pathways working and growing. So I plan to constantly engage with the learning resources of the course. This could be done in multiple ways such as stretching out the work throughout the week to make sure I don't do everything in the first half of the week and do nothing the other half, negating all the work I've done in the first place! Even if I do manage to finish everything early, even adding bite size exercises to my routine to make sure I have something to keep afloat especially on the weekend can go a long way!

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Consistently and regularly engaging in difficult or challenging activities will give your brain plenty to feed off of to keep itself growing and improving. Prioritizing sleep and mindfulness is also a key to being in the right place at all times to keep yourself able to take on these difficult activities. You wouldn't be able to take on these massive tasks if you feel like a zombie from your 2 hours of sleep or feel like your life is crashing around you...

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

As I said before, constantly giving yourself difficult learning opportunities leads to a ton of brain growth and is a hundred times more preferred to the other option which is just letting your brain deteriorate and your knowledge shrinking... but to be able to move forward in the first place you need to be in the right headspace. That's where a growth mindset comes into place! Being able to recognize and keep yourself motivated by the fact your able to always expand your knowledge and capabilities with the right approach and enough work is the start you need to get it done in the first place. Unlocking this mindset is key to being able to take on this challenging process and will lead to so many opportunities and possibilities!

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Going through highschool, I had plenty of teachers who promoted a growth mindset classroom. However, I didn't value my education let alone my growth in that time so I just kind of brushed it off. But after highschool I learnt the hard way how much a good education and the skills you learn within that education really matter. Now faced with the same concepts I was shown in highschool, I can see how much it matters for my growth and my future and it's able to resonate a lot more. What's changed is that this time around I genuinely plan to implement this approach in my learning process and hope it's able to bring me to as many learning opportunities as possible.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

As I said before, I plan to spread out my work so my brain always has something to feed off on and is constantly growing. But it's also key to note now, that those activities I'll be giving myself, need to be difficult and challenging. While doing something I'm already comfortable is technically beneficial and helps my brain not deteriorate. It'll be a lot more nicer to have the opportunity to have my knowledge grow, rather than be stagnant!
