Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a wellbeing model desgined to provide a Māori perspective on health that consists of...

taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
taha whānau (family wellbeing)
and Whenua (land and roots)

Below is my plan to stay connected with these 4 walls and the Whenua throughout my learning journey!

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

I plan to go on a run every day (taking rest days of course) with every second day being more of a walk/jog day. I've already started to try to implement this within my life but I want to make a full and official commintment here!

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

I'm planning on going to the Wellington Saints basketball games with my mum as regularly as I can, but also I've been learning to drive and I plan to get more practice in as I can as my grandad has been teaching me and that connection and experince means alot to him.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Keeping a solid and healthy sleep schedule, esspecially coming into the bootcamp part of the course, is going to be key to my learning process so I plan on going to sleep earlier (than normal) and waking up consistently at a resonable time.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

I'm not much of a spiritual person however I think being grateful for this world we live in and seeing the positive and beatutiful side of it can come under this wall. So therefore, I want to find more opportunities to just go on strolls outside, taking my time and staying around as much nature as possible.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Just like Taha Wairua where I plan to stay connected to nature as much as possible, I would like to atleast twice a week go out with my dog to a local field just to a throw a ball and just be present in the moment with the land and the nature around me.
